Masked Face Verification with a Frozen Recognizer

A lightweight pair-head adapter, with a dedicated mask-aware recognizer as the ceiling

Course project final wrap-up

Problem

Existing face systems often keep an unmasked gallery.

The hard case is matching a masked probe to an unmasked reference.

masked-unmasked
the main verification target

Goal: improve masked verification without retraining or replacing the deployed recognizer.

Final Idea

Freeze FaceNet. Extract several occlusion-aware views. Train a small verifier head over pair-level evidence.

Inference Policy

Masked pairs

Use the learned pair head for masked-masked and masked-unmasked comparisons.

Unmasked pairs

Bypass the head and keep the original FaceNet cosine score.

This preserves the legacy recognizer where masks are not involved.

Synthetic-Train Extension

+0.0014
masked-unmasked ROC-AUC gain from LFW synthetic training

Train: 1,000 LFW synthetic masked/unmasked identities.

Evaluate: 80 real RMFRD masked/unmasked identities.

Result: 0.7900 FaceNet baseline to 0.7914 pair-head masked-only policy.

This is useful as a negative transfer result: simple synthetic masks did not replace target-domain pair supervision.

What Did Not Work

  • Fixed and learned score fusion were unstable.
  • Embedding adapters produced small or inconsistent gains.
  • Partial fine-tuning helped, but added more risk and still trailed.
  • Scratch-trained periocular head was weaker: 0.6358 masked-unmasked ROC-AUC on seed 42.
  • LFW synthetic-mask training transferred only marginally: +0.0014 ROC-AUC on real RMFRD evaluation.

Claim

A lightweight pair-level adapter can recover meaningful masked-unmasked verification performance for an existing unmasked recognizer.

It does not beat a purpose-built mask-aware recognizer. That model is the ceiling and comparison point.

Takeaways

  • Best final direction: frozen FaceNet + pair-head verifier.
  • Main gain: +0.0604 and +0.0273 masked-unmasked ROC-AUC across two held-out splits.
  • Unmasked path is preserved exactly by bypassing the head.
  • Dedicated mask-aware models remain stronger, as expected.
